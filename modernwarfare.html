Modern Warfare 2: Different Aim Assist Types Explained

Unsure of the meaning behind the different types of Aim Assist? Fret not, because this guide has you covered.

One of the major reasons why Chainsaw Man is so incredible is its different Devils and Devil Hunters with unique powers and devil contracts. While the main protagonist, Denji, uses chainsaw abilities, there are others who use the power of sharks, bombs, blood, dolls, and other fascinating things.

Millions of people around the globe are jumping into Call Of Duty: Modern Warfare 2 to battle it out and come out on top. Call Of Duty has always been popular on PC, and while traditionally mouse and keyboard are used for PC gaming, the majority of the player base tends to use a controller. Modern Warfare 2 carries this trend and the game goes one step further to introduce different aim assist types for controller players to choose from.

Choosing the right aim assist type to use may prove to be a difficult task, especially if you are unsure of the difference between each option. So, whether you are one of the many Modern Warfare 2 players on Playstation and Xbox, or are a veteran looking to switch from mouse and keyboard to controller, here is the different aim assist settings in Call Of Duty: Modern Warfare 2 explained.

10/10 Beam

There are currently four different types of aim assist settings in Modern Warfare 2, each of which provides a different functionality to the game's aim assist system. These are the following:

  • Default
  • Precision
  • Focusing
  • Black Ops

When players think of the functionality of aim assist, they think of the typical built-in feature that slows down a player's crosshairs when they pass onto an enemy. This is exactly how the Default aim assists setting functions and has been around since the early Call Of Duty games, thus this option will most likely appear to be the most familiar among all the aim assist types.

Precision aim assist functions much like Default but goes a level deeper. The way Precision aim assist functions by providing a stronger slow-down of the player's crosshair but will only take effect when a player's aim is much closer to an opponent. Because of this, Precision is recommended for players with a high level of accuracy, mostly Assault Rifle, Marksman Rifle, and Sniper users.

Focusing is the third option and, much like the Precision option, provides a strong slow-down of the player's crosshair when targeting an opponent. However, this will only take effect when narrowly missing the target, making it an effective choice for players who are new to analog aiming. Therefore, if you have very little experience with playing on a controller but still want to try it out, then this option is the best choice for you.

The final option is Black Ops aim assist, and while it functions very much like the Default option, this version is more reminiscent of the aim assist present in previous Treyarch Call Of Duty titles such as Call Of Duty: Black Ops Cold War. If you favor Treyarch's take on aim assist over Infinity Ward's, then you might want to consider switching to this option.
